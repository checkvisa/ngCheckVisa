Who should use this web service?

Any non-United States citizens/green card holders who plan to apply US visa, or have already interviewed at a US embassy and have been notified that the visa needs additional “administrative processing” can use this service.

What is “check” of Visa?

“Check” is an unofficial name for administrative processing during the process of US visa application reviewing. In general, an applicant that receives a notification that your visa application requires additional administrative processing is said to be “checked”.

Why does my application get checked?

Common reasons for “check” include sensitive academic majors or directions, names, countries of origins, provinces, etc.

The visa officer already told me that I should wait three (or four, etc.) weeks, should I still use this web service to predict the waiting time for my case?

Usually the applicant will be notified that they should wait 3 to 4 weeks. But this time is never accurate, and is dependent on a number of factors. By reviewing the information of your specific case, we may provide a more accurate estimation of your waiting time based on machine learning algorithms.

What are the most important factors that determine the length of check waiting time?

We found that Visa type (F1, J1, etc.) and academic major are two most important features that influence waiting time. We are working on providing more available features to capture the nuance between different cases.

Is the waiting time predictable? Doesn’t the time vary from person to person?

Yes and yes. Our statistical analysis shows that the waiting time is strongly dependent on the visa type, student’s major, country of origin, etc. When you submit your case information that includes such information, we use machine learning models to predict the waiting time specifically for your waiting time. This prediction takes into consideration not only the information you submitted, but also the recent trend of average waiting time.

How accurate is your prediction?

Our prediction explains 30% of the variance of visa waiting time. We are working on providing you a time range, so that with 95% chance the waiting time of your “checked” visa will fall into this range.

How do you predict the waiting time?

We collect previous cases, and train machine learning models on these cases, and apply such models to new queries to make prediction. The underlying assumption is that people whose cases are similar (in terms of time, location, applicant, etc.) would have similar waiting time.

Do you have internal source within the US government?

No. All of the information we gather are open on the Internet.

What is your data source?

Initially we crawled part of our data from checkee.info, which is a great website that helps peoples who got “checked”. As the website grows, we are starting collecting cases by ourselves through the website.

How is this website related to checkee.info?

We are not related to checkee.info, which is another website that provides US visa check information. Part of our data is crawled from checkee.info, and they are used to improve our prediction accuracy. We recommend you to look at checkee.info to read other applicants’ comments and experiences.

Do you collect my personal information?

No. All the data collected are anonymous - i.e., we do not collect your names, ID number, phone number and any other private information. For prediction accuracy we do plan to ask users for age (birthday) information, but all such fields are optional during the usage of our service, and the submission of cases is completely voluntarily. We will never attempt to match the case information we collected to any person, and we have make every effort to protect our data not be attacked by anyone who is evil.

How can I use this prediction result?

The prediction is a reference. You can refer to the prediction result to book a flight at a relatively “safe” time, but you should NOT rely on the prediction result in any situation, as it is not perfectly accurate and it will never be.
